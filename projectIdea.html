At the end of each week at an agreed time set by the project manager (who plays an important part in team projects, we‚Äôll talk about this later), everyone in the team can get their drinks and ToDoTopian avatars ready for a Battle. How Tasks are Displayed

As mentioned earlier, ToDoTopia displays tasks in a list by default. But views can be changed from Lists to Kanban Boards, Calendars, Timelines and People views.

Every task contains three properties: Date, Progress & Assigned.

Date has a start and an end, or just a due date. Either way, it will be used to display in the Calendar and Timeline views.

Progress contains To-Do, In Progress, Under Review & Complete by default, but these can be changed from project to project. This property is used for the Kanban Board view, but is also seen on other views as an icon.

Assigned is a property that assigns a team member or team members to a task. This is used to organise tasks in the People view, but is also displayed as an icon on the task in every other view.

Lists are displayed in, well, a list. Subtasks are viewed in the same view and can be toggled to show or hide on a task to task basis. By default, they are all toggled open (meaning, you will see every task).

Kanban Boards are self-explanatory. You drag tasks from one state to progression to another. Any task with a progress property will be displayed. If a task hasn‚Äôt had entered a progress status, it will not be displayed on the board (unless it is a sub-task and its parent task has a progress status, in this case it will be displayed under the parent task on the board and indented a bit).

Calendars are a month at a time and display tasks either with one date on that day, or with a start and end date stretching over multiple days. A Timeline works the same but is a continuous line rather than a month grid.

The People view lists all the team members and their assigned tasks. This will be displayed as a gallery, and you will click on that team members avatar to view their tasks as a drop down.

Why Gamification?

Why Gamification. Well, that‚Äôs pretty simple... it makes work FUN! Along side this, search the web and you will find studies showing the benefits of gamification in the workplace (including increased productivity!).

We wanted to combine the concept of gamification with already really successful to-do list frameworks. That‚Äôs why the use of lists, Kanban boards, timelines, etc are so important to ToDoTopia.
