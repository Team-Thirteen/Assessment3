Starts by showing the Azure DevOps Kanban Board… So, one of the things we are working with day to day is Azure DevOps, which is a Microsoft product. It’s an Agile environment hosted in the cloud. We start sprints in columns and people pull the tasks into active and once it’s done it gets reviewed and tested. Generally, people (developers) collaborate on them (tasks in the Kanban board), and the numbers allocated to the tasks are sizes. So, we don't give estimates in terms of time, we give it in terms of complexity. So, five is more complex than a three, which is more complex than two. We measure how many points we did and that sprint plus the average, and that's what we used to plan the next sprint. Scrum is an approach to organizing teams around a collective goal, and the idea is that you do some work. You reflect on it and then you re-plan it. It’s not a software approach but an organizational approach for our team to work effectively. The idea is that you do some work, you share that work that you've done with stakeholders, organizations, your team, and you reflect on it and use the output of that work to decide what to do next. So traditionally projects would require you to go and build some code for six months to twelve months and then at the end of that period if you realize it's wrong, it could be disastrous and is a bad way of doing things.
